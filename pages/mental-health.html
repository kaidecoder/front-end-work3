Types of Mental Illness Symptoms Causes
Mood disorders(like bipolar or major depression) unusual or illogical thoughts Genetic factors — having a close family member with a mental illness can increase the chance that you might get a mental illness. However, just because one family member has a mental illness doesn't mean that others will.
Anxiety disorders unreasonable anger or irritability Drug and alcohol abuse — illicit drug use can trigger a manic episode (bipolar disorder) or an episode of psychosis. Drugs such as cocaine, marijuana and amphetamines can cause paranoia.
Personality disorders poor concentration and memory, not being able to follow a conversation Other biological factors — some medical conditions or hormonal changes can cause mental health problems.
Psychotic disorders(such as Schizophrenia) hearing voices that no one else can hear Early life environment — negative childhood experiences can increase the risk of some mental illnesses. Examples of negative childhood experiences are abuse or neglect.
Eating disorders increased or decreased sleep Trauma and stress — in adulthood, traumatic life events or ongoing stress can increase the risk of mental illness. Issues such as social isolation, domestic violence, relationship breakdown, financial or work problems can impact on mental health. Traumatic experiences such as living in a war zone can increase the risk of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).
Trauma-related disorders(such as Post Traumatic Stress Disorder) increased or low appetite, or preoccupation with control over food, calories or excessive exercise Personality factors — some traits such as perfectionism or low self-esteem can increase the risk of depression or anxiety.
